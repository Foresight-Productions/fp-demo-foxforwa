Her Platform

Five Priorities.
One Mission.

Teresa Fox's campaign is built around the industries and communities that define the Olympic Peninsula, Kitsap, and South Sound. No platitudes — just real commitments to the workers and families who make this region run.

01
Maritime Jobs

The Puget Sound shipyards and naval facilities are the economic backbone of WA-6. Teresa will fight to protect defense contracting, expand apprenticeship programs, and keep skilled trades jobs right here in Kitsap — not offshored or downsized out of existence.

02
Timber Economy

The Olympic Peninsula's timber industry supports thousands of families and rural communities. Teresa will push back on federal overreach that shuts down responsible harvesting and will advocate for sustainable forestry policies that balance environmental stewardship with economic reality.

03
Military & Veterans

With Naval Base Kitsap as the district's largest employer, Teresa understands what's at stake for active-duty families and veterans. She'll fight to fully fund VA services, improve transition support for separating servicemembers, and ensure Bremerton's military community gets the congressional attention it deserves.

04
Small Business

Teresa has seen firsthand how regulations and rising costs squeeze local business owners in communities like Sequim, Port Angeles, and Port Orchard. She'll champion lower regulatory burdens, oppose tax hikes on small employers, and bring a pro-business voice to Congress that actually knows the difference between a Main Street shop and a Fortune 500 boardroom.

05
Working Families

The cost of housing, healthcare, and child care has made it harder than ever for families in WA-6 to get ahead. Teresa will push for workforce housing solutions, oppose policies that inflate energy costs, and make sure Washington's working families — not lobbyists — have a seat at the table.

About Teresa

A Worker. An Expert. A Neighbor.

Teresa Fox grew up understanding what it means to work hard for what you have. As an occupational safety professional and emergency manager, she's spent her career in the shipyards, naval facilities, and offshore operations that define this region — not studying them from a distance, but working inside them.

She's also served with the Washington State Department of Labor and Industries and taught at Olympic College. Teresa isn't a career politician. She's someone who has spent decades protecting workers, and now she wants to protect the communities those workers live in.
